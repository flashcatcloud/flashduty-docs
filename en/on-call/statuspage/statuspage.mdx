Service interruptions are inevitable, but communication efficiency can be systematically improved.

When service status fluctuates, relying on manual emails or instant messages for notifications can no longer meet enterprise requirements for information timeliness and consistency. Flashduty Status Page provides a standardized, unified information publishing window to ensure that both internal and external stakeholders maintain real-time awareness of service status.

<Tip>
Subscribe to the [Flashduty Official Status Page](https://status.flashcat.cloud/flashduty) to receive Flashduty service status updates first.
</Tip>

## Status Page Types

To address different audience needs, Flashduty offers two types of status pages:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Public Status Page">
    **For customers and partners**

    - Open to all public internet users, accessible without sign in
    - Help enterprises provide real-time, accurate service status updates to customers during incidents
    - Proactively deliver critical information, ease customer anxiety, and build a professional brand image
    - Users can subscribe to event updates via **email**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Internal Status Page">
    **For internal organization employees**

    - Accessible only to internal organization users, requires Flashduty account sign in
    - Provides a unified, real-time service status view
    - Management can understand overall system health without diving into technical details
    - Sales and customer support teams can accurately understand system status
    - Users can receive real-time notifications via **IM integration** (Feishu/Lark, Dingtalk, WeCom, Slack)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between Status Page and Uptime Monitors (Uptime Kuma)?">
    Both provide dashboards showing service availability, but with different purposes:

    | Dimension | Status Page | Uptime Monitor |
    | --- | --- | --- |
    | Audience | External customers & cross-department communication | Internal ops & technical teams |
    | Deployment | Third-party hosted | Self-hosted |
    | Monitoring Method | Relies on event reporting | Self-managed monitoring probes |
    | Focus | Transparent internal/external communication | Alert notifications to technical teams |

    **In short**: Use uptime monitors for service liveness checks; use Flashduty Status Page for official service status dashboards.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can manage the status page?">
    Flashduty Status Page follows the RBAC permission model. Administrators can assign the following roles:
    - **Status Page Management**: Create, edit, and delete status pages, manage subscriptions
    - **Status Page Event Management**: Publish, edit, and delete events
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I publish events?">
    Currently, events need to be manually declared in the management page, with **event templates** supported for quick publishing.

    <Info>
    Workflow support is planned for the future, allowing automatic event publishing on relevant status pages through preset rules.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I subscribe to specific services only?">
    Yes. Users can flexibly choose their subscription scope, subscribing only to specific services or events to reduce unnecessary notification noise.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is service availability calculated?">
    - **Partial Outage** or **Full Outage** counts as downtime
    - Maintenance time is **not included** in availability statistics
    - Calculated based on the most recent quarter's data window
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I backfill historical data?">
    Yes. Through **Retrospective Events**, you can declare incidents or maintenance that have already occurred, accurately set the time and affected components. Retrospective events are included in availability statistics.
  </Accordion>

    For special requirements, please contact customer service.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
